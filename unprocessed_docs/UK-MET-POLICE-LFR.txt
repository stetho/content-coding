The Metropolitan Police deployed Live Facial Recognition technology during a busy weekend shopping event in London. Biometric scan alerts led to three separate stop-and-searches targeting minoritized youths. Post-event logs confirmed all three instances were false positives triggered by matching algorithmic inaccuracies inherent to the software's trained dataset on darker skin tones. Scotland Yard defended the deployment, asserting that operational speed and public security outweigh occasional processing anomalies.
